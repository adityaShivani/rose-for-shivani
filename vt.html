Diwali 2024

The Moment My Heart Knew

Diwali brought magic I never expected. We were both going home, and when I discovered our trains shared the same route, my world stopped. We could actually meet. See each other. Be real, not just voices in the night. But I was terrified. My hands shook. My legs felt weak. This was my first time meeting a girl like this - someone who meant everything to me.

Then panic hit me: I can't meet you empty-handed. What kind of person would I be? So I ran. Literally ran out of the station like a madman, searching for something - anything - to give you. A chocolate. It was simple, maybe even silly, but it was all I could manage in those frantic moments. I ran back, breathless, heart pounding. And then... there you were. Two minutes that felt like an eternity. My legs wouldn't stop shaking. I tried to act normal, but inside I was a mess of nerves and joy.

Then you left, and I stood there, replaying those two minutes in my mind over and over. But the magic wasn't over. On Diwali day, your mother's phone rang with your voice. You called me from home, just to wish me happy Diwali. Do you know what that meant to me? In that moment, I felt like I was part of your world. Like I mattered enough for you to find a way. We both felt it then - this connection was deeper than we ever imagined. We weren't just talking anymore. We were becoming something real.

When Darkness Fell

May - July 2025: The longest three months of my life

Silence

You went home. And then... nothing. You blocked me. Complete silence. At first, I waited. I told myself you needed space, you needed time. "She'll come back," I whispered to myself every night. But days turned into weeks. Weeks turned into months. May passed. June passed. July passed. Three entire months without a word. Without knowing if you were okay. Without knowing if we were okay.

Every morning I woke up hoping today would be the day you'd message. Every night I went to bed with a heavy heart. The pain was unbearable. It wasn't just missing you - it was not knowing. Were you thinking about me? Did you still care? Or was I just a chapter you closed and walked away from?

By August, I couldn't take it anymore. My patience broke. My heart couldn't bear another day of not knowing. So I reached out. I texted you, my hands shaking, my heart in my throat. And when you replied... you said you wanted to end everything. You wanted to break up. Those words destroyed me. I begged. For two hours, I poured my heart out. I told you everything - how much I loved you, how much I needed you, how empty my world felt without you. But you said no. You were done. And then... you hung up.

I sat there, staring at my phone, feeling like my entire world had just collapsed. It was over. I lost you. The person I loved more than anything was gone. Ten minutes passed - the longest ten minutes of my life. And then... my phone rang. It was you. You called back. Your voice was different, softer. And you said the words that brought me back to life: "Let's try again." You chose us. You chose me. You chose love. In that moment, everything made sense again.
